FORKLIFT SAFETY AND OPERATION PROCEDURE
Document No: HSP-331
Revision: 3
Effective Date: January 15, 2024
Department: Health, Safety & Environment

================================================================================
1. PURPOSE
================================================================================

The purpose of this procedure is to establish minimum safety requirements for the
operation, maintenance, and inspection of powered industrial trucks (forklifts)
at all company facilities. This procedure applies to all employees, contractors,
and temporary workers who operate or work near forklifts.

================================================================================
2. SCOPE
================================================================================

This procedure covers the following types of powered industrial trucks:
- Counterbalanced forklifts (sit-down and stand-up)
- Reach trucks
- Order pickers
- Pallet jacks (powered)
- Rough terrain forklifts

This procedure does not cover manual pallet jacks or hand trucks.

================================================================================
3. REFERENCES
================================================================================

- OSHA 29 CFR 1910.178 – Powered Industrial Trucks

================================================================================
4. DEFINITIONS
================================================================================

4.1 Powered Industrial Truck (PIT): A mobile, power-propelled truck used to
    carry, push, pull, lift, stack, or tier materials.

4.2 Competent Person: An individual who has been trained and has demonstrated
    proficiency in forklift operation.

4.3 Designated Area: An area specifically assigned for forklift operations,
    loading/unloading, and material storage.

================================================================================
5. RESPONSIBILITIES
================================================================================

5.1 Management:
    - Ensure all operators are trained and certified
    - Provide properly maintained forklifts
    - Enforce compliance with this procedure

5.2 Supervisors:
    - Monitor daily operations for safe practices
    - Conduct periodic safety observations
    - Remove defective equipment from service

5.3 Operators:
    - Complete required training before operating any forklift
    - Perform pre-shift inspections
    - Report any defects or unsafe conditions immediately
    - Operate forklifts in accordance with this procedure

================================================================================
6. TRAINING AND CERTIFICATION
================================================================================

6.1 Initial Training:
    All new forklift operators must complete a training program that includes:
    a) Classroom/online instruction covering:
       - Truck-related topics (controls, instrumentation, steering, visibility)
       - Operating environment (surface conditions, ramps, hazardous areas)
       - Safe operating practices
    b) Practical (hands-on) training under direct supervision
    c) Evaluation of the operator's performance in the workplace

6.2 Certification:
    Upon successful completion of training, operators will receive a certification
    card/license valid for three (3) years. Certification records shall be
    maintained by the Safety Department.

6.3 Refresher Training:
    Refresher training and re-evaluation shall be conducted:
    - Every three (3) years
    - When an operator is observed operating unsafely
    - After an accident or near-miss involving a forklift
    - When assigned to a different type of forklift
    - When workplace conditions change significantly

6.4 Only trained and certified operators are permitted to operate forklifts.
    Unauthorized operation of a forklift is grounds for disciplinary action.

================================================================================
7. PRE-OPERATIONAL INSPECTION
================================================================================

7.1 Operators must perform a pre-shift inspection before operating a forklift.
    The inspection shall include, but is not limited to:
    - Fluid levels (oil, coolant, hydraulic fluid)
    - Tires and wheels (condition, inflation)
    - Forks (cracks, straightness, locking pins)
    - Mast and chains (condition, lubrication)
    - Hydraulic system (leaks, operation)
    - Brakes (service brake, parking brake)
    - Steering
    - Horn
    - Lights (headlights, warning lights, strobe)
    - Backup alarm
    - Seat belt condition

7.2 Any deficiencies found during the pre-shift inspection must be reported
    to the supervisor. A forklift with safety-related defects shall be removed
    from service and tagged out until repairs are completed.

7.3 A written inspection checklist shall be completed and submitted to the
    supervisor at the beginning of each shift.

================================================================================
8. SAFE OPERATING PROCEDURES
================================================================================

8.1 General Rules:
    a) Always wear a seatbelt when operating a forklift equipped with one.
    b) Keep arms and legs inside the operator compartment at all times.
    c) Do not exceed the rated load capacity of the forklift.
    d) Travel with forks lowered (approximately 4 inches from the ground).
    e) Sound the horn at intersections, blind corners, and when approaching
       pedestrians.
    f) Maintain a safe following distance from other vehicles.
    g) Yield to pedestrians at all times.

8.2 Load Handling:
    a) Center the load on the forks as close to the mast as possible.
    b) Tilt the mast back to stabilize the load before traveling.
    c) Do not raise or lower loads while traveling.
    d) Ensure loads do not obstruct forward visibility; travel in reverse
       if necessary.

8.3 Speed and Travel:
    a) Observe posted speed limits in all areas.
    b) Slow down on wet or slippery surfaces.
    c) Keep a safe distance from edges of ramps, platforms, and docks.
    d) Do not drive on surfaces that cannot support the loaded weight.

8.4 Prohibited Activities:
    a) Do not allow riders on the forklift unless it is equipped with a
       passenger seat.
    b) Do not use the forklift to push or tow other vehicles without
       proper attachments.
    c) Do not raise people on the forks unless using an approved work platform
       with appropriate fall protection.
    d) Do not operate a forklift while under the influence of drugs or alcohol.
    e) Do not use a cell phone while operating a forklift.

================================================================================
9. FUELING AND CHARGING
================================================================================

9.1 Propane-Powered Forklifts:
    - Refuel in designated, well-ventilated areas
    - No smoking within 25 feet of refueling area
    - Inspect propane tanks for damage before connecting

9.2 Electric Forklifts:
    - Charge batteries in designated charging areas with adequate ventilation
    - Wear appropriate PPE (face shield, rubber gloves) when handling batteries
    - Keep open flames and sparks away from charging areas

9.3 Diesel/Gasoline Forklifts:
    - Shut off the engine before refueling
    - No smoking during refueling

================================================================================
10. PARKING AND SHUTDOWN
================================================================================

10.1 When parking a forklift:
     a) Park in designated parking areas only
     b) Lower forks completely to the ground
     c) Set the parking brake
     d) Turn off the engine/power
     e) Remove the key (if applicable)

10.2 Never park a forklift on an incline unless wheels are blocked.

================================================================================
11. MAINTENANCE
================================================================================

11.1 Only qualified maintenance personnel shall perform repairs on forklifts.

11.2 Forklifts shall receive scheduled preventive maintenance as recommended
     by the manufacturer.

11.3 Modifications or additions to forklifts shall not be made without prior
     written approval from the manufacturer.

================================================================================
12. PEDESTRIAN SAFETY
================================================================================

12.1 Pedestrians must stay clear of forklift operating areas.

12.2 Designated pedestrian walkways shall be clearly marked in areas where
     forklifts operate.

12.3 Operators must make eye contact with pedestrians before proceeding.

================================================================================
13. INCIDENT REPORTING
================================================================================

13.1 All forklift-related incidents, including near-misses, must be reported
     to the supervisor immediately.

13.2 Incidents shall be investigated and documented in accordance with the
     company's incident investigation procedure.

13.3 Corrective actions shall be implemented to prevent recurrence.

================================================================================
14. RECORDKEEPING
================================================================================

14.1 The following records shall be maintained:
     - Operator training and certification records
     - Pre-shift inspection checklists
     - Maintenance records
     - Incident reports

14.2 Training records shall be retained for the duration of employment
     plus three (3) years.
