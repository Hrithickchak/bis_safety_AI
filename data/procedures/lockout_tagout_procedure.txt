LOCKOUT / TAGOUT (LOTO) PROCEDURE
Control of Hazardous Energy
Document No: HSP-210
Revision: 4
Effective Date: March 1, 2024
Department: Health, Safety & Environment

================================================================================
1. PURPOSE
================================================================================

This procedure establishes the minimum requirements for the lockout/tagout of
energy isolating devices whenever maintenance or servicing is performed on
machines and equipment. It is used to ensure that the machine or equipment is
stopped, isolated from all potentially hazardous energy sources, and locked out
before employees perform any servicing or maintenance.

================================================================================
2. SCOPE
================================================================================

This procedure applies to all employees, contractors, and temporary workers who
service or maintain machines and equipment where the unexpected energization,
start-up, or release of stored energy could cause injury. This includes:
- Electrical energy
- Mechanical energy
- Hydraulic/pneumatic energy
- Thermal energy
- Chemical energy
- Gravitational energy (stored/potential)

This procedure does not apply to:
- Cord-and-plug connected equipment where the plug is under exclusive control
  of the employee performing the service
- Hot tap operations on pressurized systems with documented procedures

================================================================================
3. REFERENCES
================================================================================

- OSHA 29 CFR 1910.147 – The Control of Hazardous Energy (Lockout/Tagout)
- ANSI/ASSE Z244.1 – Control of Hazardous Energy

================================================================================
4. DEFINITIONS
================================================================================

4.1 Affected Employee: An employee whose job requires them to operate or use a
    machine or equipment on which servicing or maintenance is being performed
    under lockout/tagout.

4.2 Authorized Employee: A person who locks out or tags out machines or equipment
    in order to perform servicing or maintenance on that machine or equipment.

4.3 Energy Isolating Device: A mechanical device that physically prevents the
    transmission or release of energy (e.g., circuit breaker, disconnect switch,
    line valve, block).

4.4 Lockout: The placement of a lockout device on an energy isolating device
    to ensure that the equipment being controlled cannot be operated until the
    lockout device is removed.

4.5 Tagout: The placement of a tagout device on an energy isolating device
    to indicate that the equipment shall not be operated until the tagout
    device is removed.

================================================================================
5. RESPONSIBILITIES
================================================================================

5.1 Management:
    - Provide lockout/tagout devices and hardware
    - Ensure all authorized and affected employees are trained
    - Enforce compliance with this procedure

5.2 Authorized Employees:
    - Follow all steps of the lockout/tagout procedure
    - Apply personal locks and tags to energy isolating devices
    - Verify energy isolation before beginning work

5.3 Affected Employees:
    - Do not attempt to restart or re-energize machines that are locked/tagged out
    - Notify authorized employees of any concerns

================================================================================
6. TRAINING
================================================================================

6.1 All authorized employees shall receive training that covers:
    - Recognition of applicable hazardous energy sources
    - Type and magnitude of energy available in the workplace
    - Methods and means necessary for energy isolation and control

6.2 All affected employees shall be instructed in:
    - The purpose and use of the lockout/tagout procedure
    - The prohibition against attempting to restart locked/tagged out equipment

6.3 Retraining shall be provided:
    - When there is a change in job assignments, machines, or processes
    - When a periodic inspection reveals inadequacies
    - At least every two (2) years

6.4 Training records shall include employee name, date of training, and
    trainer identity.

================================================================================
7. LOCKOUT/TAGOUT PROCEDURE STEPS
================================================================================

7.1 PREPARATION:
    a) Identify all energy sources associated with the machine/equipment
    b) Identify the type and magnitude of each energy source
    c) Identify the energy isolating device(s) for each source
    d) Obtain the necessary lockout/tagout devices

7.2 NOTIFICATION:
    a) Notify all affected employees that the machine/equipment will be
       shut down and locked/tagged out
    b) Explain the reason for the lockout/tagout

7.3 SHUTDOWN:
    a) Shut down the machine/equipment using normal operating procedures
    b) Ensure all operators are clear of the machine

7.4 ISOLATION:
    a) Operate the energy isolating device(s) to isolate the machine from
       its energy source(s)
    b) Dissipate or restrain any stored or residual energy
       (e.g., capacitors, springs, elevated components, rotating flywheels,
       hydraulic/pneumatic pressure, steam, hot water)

7.5 LOCKOUT/TAGOUT APPLICATION:
    a) Each authorized employee shall apply their personal lock and tag to
       each energy isolating device
    b) Tags must clearly identify the employee, date, and reason for lockout
    c) Locks must be individually keyed – no master keys or duplicate keys

7.6 VERIFICATION:
    a) After applying locks/tags, verify that isolation is effective by
       attempting to start the machine using normal controls
    b) Return controls to the "off" or "neutral" position after verification
    c) Test for zero energy state (e.g., voltage tester for electrical,
       pressure gauge for hydraulic/pneumatic)

7.7 PERFORM WORK:
    a) Proceed with the servicing or maintenance activity

================================================================================
8. RESTORING EQUIPMENT TO SERVICE
================================================================================

8.1 Before removing locks/tags:
    a) Inspect the work area to ensure all tools and materials are removed
    b) Ensure all guards and safety devices are reinstalled
    c) Verify that all employees are clear of the machine

8.2 Remove locks and tags:
    a) Only the authorized employee who applied the lock/tag may remove it
    b) Notify affected employees that the machine will be re-energized

8.3 Re-energize:
    a) Re-energize the machine following normal startup procedures
    b) Verify the machine operates correctly

================================================================================
9. GROUP LOCKOUT/TAGOUT
================================================================================

9.1 When multiple employees are working on the same machine:
    a) A designated lead person shall coordinate the lockout/tagout
    b) Each authorized employee shall apply their own personal lock
    c) The primary lock shall be removed last after all personal locks
       are removed

================================================================================
10. SHIFT CHANGE PROCEDURES
================================================================================

10.1 During shift changes, continuity of lockout/tagout protection shall be
     maintained. The oncoming shift employee shall apply their lock before
     the outgoing employee removes theirs.

================================================================================
11. PERIODIC INSPECTION
================================================================================

11.1 A periodic inspection of the energy control procedure shall be conducted
     at least annually.

11.2 The inspection shall be performed by an authorized employee who is not
     using the energy control procedure being inspected.

11.3 The inspection shall include a review of each authorized employee's
     responsibilities under the procedure.

11.4 Inspection results shall be documented.

================================================================================
12. LOCK REMOVAL BY OTHER THAN THE APPLYING EMPLOYEE
================================================================================

12.1 In the rare event that an authorized employee's lock must be removed by
     someone other than the employee who applied it:
     a) Verify that the employee who applied the lock is not at the facility
     b) Make reasonable efforts to contact the employee
     c) Ensure the employee knows the lock has been removed before they
        resume work at the facility
     d) The removal must be authorized by the facility manager

================================================================================
13. RECORDKEEPING
================================================================================

13.1 The following records shall be maintained:
     - LOTO training records
     - Periodic inspection records
     - Equipment-specific energy control procedures

13.2 Records shall be retained for a minimum of three (3) years.
