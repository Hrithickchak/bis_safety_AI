Executive Summary

**Executive Summary: Safety Compliance Audit of Procedure Hsp 331** This report details the findings of a comprehensive safety compliance audit performed on Procedure Hsp 331, evaluated against OSHA 29 CFR 1910.178, ANSI/ITSDF B56.1, CSA B335, and related fall protection and fire safety standards. The procedure achieved an overall compliance score of **38.3%**, with 10 items passing, 17 failing, and 3 partially meeting the requirements. This score indicates significant regulatory gaps that require immediate remedial action to ensure operator safety and legal compliance. The most critical deficiencies identified relate to equipment stability and load management, which present a high risk of tip-overs or falling loads. Specifically, the procedure fails to mandate the presence and use of load charts for telehandlers (Variable Reach Forklifts), a critical safety violation. Furthermore, the document lacks explicit instructions for operating on grades exceeding 10%—where loads must remain upgrade—and fails to define proper mast tilt protocols for stabilizing loads during travel. These omissions are classified as High to Critical severity because they directly impact the physical stability of the vehicle and the safety of the operator and bystanders. Despite these gaps, Procedure Hsp 331 demonstrates a solid foundation in administrative training requirements. The procedure is fully compliant regarding the qualifications of "Competent Trainers" and correctly identifies the three-year interval for operator recertification. Additionally, it accurately incorporates OSHA triggers for refresher training following an accident or near-miss and maintains high standards for securing transport vehicles (e.g., setting brakes and wheel blocks) during loading and unloading operations. To bring Procedure Hsp 331 into full compliance, the following actions are recommended in order of priority: 1. **Immediate Update:** Amend section 5.3.2 to mandate that legible load charts be maintained in the cab of all telehandlers and consulted for every lift. 2. **Operational Safety:** Insert specific clauses in section 5.3.1 regarding ramp/grade operations (load upgrade for >10% inclines) and mast tilt requirements for load stabilization. 3. **Technical Specifics:** Update "Safe Operation" sections to include the "three truck lengths" following distance rule and the requirement to cross railroad tracks diagonally. 4. **Accessibility:** Add a requirement that manufacturer operating manuals must be stored in a weather-resistant compartment on the equipment at all times. The current risk posture for Procedure Hsp 331 is **High**. While the administrative and training-cycle elements are well-defined, the procedure lacks the technical operational depth required by OSHA and CSA to prevent catastrophic equipment failure or operator error. Until the identified gaps in load handling and incline operations are addressed, the organization remains exposed to significant liability and increased risk of workplace incidents.
